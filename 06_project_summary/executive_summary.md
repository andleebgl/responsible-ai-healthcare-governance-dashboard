# Executive Summary

## Project Title
Responsible AI Governance and Healthcare Use Case Dashboard

## Overview
This project brings together my experience in NHS digital systems, responsible AI research, LLM evaluation, and data reporting. It demonstrates how AI and data use cases can be collected, assessed, prioritised, governed, and communicated through SQL analysis, Power BI reporting, and responsible AI governance templates.

The project uses a synthetic healthcare AI use case dataset created for portfolio demonstration. The dataset reflects realistic AI, data, and automation requests that might arise in a high trust healthcare environment, including clinical note summarisation, patient triage, readmission prediction, medical coding support, and operational automation.

## What This Project Contains

### 1. Research Foundation
A research note exploring responsible AI risks in healthcare, covering hallucination in LLMs, patient data privacy, human oversight, bias in prediction systems, explainability, governance controls, and regulatory context including the EU AI Act and WHO guidance.

### 2. Synthetic Dataset
A dataset of 20 AI use cases submitted across 8 departments in a healthcare organisation, with risk assessments, governance statuses, business value scores, and 28 governance controls. A full data dictionary documents every field.

### 3. SQL Analysis
Six SQL query files covering data quality checks, use case risk analysis, bias and privacy and oversight analysis, prioritisation analysis, and benefits tracking. Each file answers specific governance questions such as which use cases are high risk, which have no owner, which are pilot ready, and where the organisation has high value requests but low policy readiness.

### 4. Power BI Dashboard
A three page dashboard visualising the AI use case pipeline, responsible AI risk profile, and prioritisation and controls. Designed for non technical stakeholders to understand the status and risk of AI adoption across the organisation.

### 5. Governance Strategy Pack
Five templates providing practical governance tools: an AI use case intake template, a responsible AI risk register, a use case prioritisation matrix, a BI governance checklist, and a benefits tracking template.

## Key Findings from the Analysis
- 20 AI use cases across 8 departments, covering LLM, predictive model, NLP, computer vision, and automation
- 40% of use cases are classified as high risk
- 25% of use cases have no assigned owner, representing a governance gap
- 7 use cases have high business value but low policy readiness
- Emergency Medicine has the highest average business value score at 8.7 across 3 use cases
- 8 use cases require human oversight and explainability
- 10 use cases have privacy risk scores of 7 or above

## Skills Demonstrated

## Why This Project Matters
AI adoption in high trust sectors requires more than technical capability. Organisations need a structured way to understand requests, identify risks, avoid duplication, assign ownership, track benefits, and communicate decisions clearly. This project demonstrates one way to build that structure.
