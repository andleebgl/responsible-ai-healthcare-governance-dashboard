# Use Case Prioritisation Matrix

## Purpose
This matrix provides a structured framework for deciding which AI use cases to progress first, based on business value, risk, and organisational readiness.

## Prioritisation Criteria

| Criterion | Description | Score Range |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Business Value | How much impact will this use case deliver? | 1 (Low) to 10 (High) |
| Overall Risk | How much risk does this use case carry? | 1 (Low) to 10 (High) |
| Policy Readiness | How ready are the organisation's policies for this use case? | 1 (Not Ready) to 10 (Fully Ready) |
| Data Readiness | Is the required data available, clean, and accessible? | 1 (Not Ready) to 10 (Fully Ready) |
| Ownership | Is there a named owner responsible for delivery? | Yes or No |

## Priority Categories

| Priority | Criteria | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Pilot Now | Business value 7+, risk score 5 or below, policy readiness 7+, owner assigned | Proceed to pilot immediately |
| Quick Win | Business value 6+, risk score 5 or below, approved status | Progress with standard governance |
| High Value but Not Ready | Business value 7+, policy readiness 4 or below | Accelerate policy work or manage expectations |
| Needs Governance Work | Under Review or Paused status, no owner or low policy readiness | Assign owner, complete risk assessment, build controls |
| Deprioritise | Business value 4 or below, or high risk with low value | Park or reject with clear reasoning |

## How to Use This Matrix

1. Score each use case against the five criteria above
2. Place it into the appropriate priority category
3. Use the action column to determine the next step
4. Review the matrix monthly at the governance board
5. Move use cases between categories as their scores change

## Worked Example

| Use Case | Business Value | Risk | Policy Readiness | Owner | Category |
|----------|---------------|------|-----------------|-------|----------|
| LLM Clinical Note Summarisation | 9 | 8 | 7 | Yes | High value, high risk — proceed with strict controls |
| Patient Feedback Sentiment Analysis | 6 | 5 | 8 | Yes | Quick Win — progress with standard governance |
| Readmission Risk Prediction | 7 | 8 | 3 | No | High Value but Not Ready — assign owner, build policy |
| Duplicate Patient Record Detection | 4 | 2 | 9 | Yes | Low value, low risk — proceed but low priority |

## Review
This matrix should be updated whenever a new use case is submitted, a risk assessment is completed, or a governance status changes.
